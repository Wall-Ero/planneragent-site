PlannerAgent — FAQ v4.1 (Canonical Set)
Every organization operates inside an operational reality.
Plans, systems, people, workflows, decisions, and AI participation all exist within that reality.
Before an organization can improve, automate, delegate, or govern anything, it must first understand what is actually happening.
PlannerAgent starts from operational reality.
It reconstructs how plans, work, decisions, systems, and intelligence participation interact over time — and reveals whether reality remains coherent with intent.

Who is PlannerAgent?
 An authority layer that helps organizations observe, govern, and safely delegate operational decisions across systems and teams.
 It continuously monitors how plans evolve in reality and structures what is happening into certifiable, governable choices that can be approved, delegated, and audited by design.
 It ensures that human intent remains in control as automation and AI tools become more powerful.
 PlannerAgent is not an AI platform.
 It is a trust and authority layer for enterprise decisions, operational work, and the governed use of artificial intelligence.

What does PlannerAgent actually do?
PlannerAgent operates as a governed operational planner across observation, structured advisory, and execution.
It:
reads operational data and continuously monitors how plans evolve in reality
makes the current situation immediately visible through operational signals
highlights deviations, risks, and decision pressure
structures the operational situation into governable, decision-ready reality
These signals help decision-makers quickly understand:
how reliable the data is
whether the plan is coherent
whether reality is aligned
how urgent it is to act
Depending on authority:
it proposes solutions that can be evaluated and approved
it executes approved or delegated actions within defined boundaries
it orchestrates systems and workflows under governance
PlannerAgent does not own decisions and does not provide generic suggestions.
It makes situations clear and, when operating under advisory or execution scope, proposes and executes actions within explicit human control.
PlannerAgent continuously reads your operational reality and exposes it through four signals:
Data Awareness  
How reliable and complete your data is
Plan Coherence  
Whether your plan is internally consistent
Reality Alignment  
Whether reality is following your plan
Decision Pressure  
How urgently action is required
These are not dashboards.
They are operational signals that help you understand, at a glance, what is happening and where intervention may be required.
PlannerAgent is designed to reduce the need for constant operational vigilance.
Many planners and managers operate in a continuous state of cognitive monitoring — repeatedly checking systems, anticipating exceptions, and mentally holding operational coherence together.
PlannerAgent continuously observes operational reality, reconstructs plan coherence, and detects meaningful deviations as they emerge.
This reduces the need to rely on permanent alertness as the primary mechanism of operational control.
Instead of continuously searching for hidden problems, decision-makers can focus attention where intervention is truly required.
You no longer need to carry the entire operational system in your head.
When something changes, PlannerAgent does not react with alerts.
It reveals the situation, structures the problem, and proposes actionable solutions.
When operating under advisory or execution authority, PlannerAgent proposes structured actions, evaluates trade-offs, and explains operational impact so that decisions can be taken with clarity.
When enabled through approval or delegation authority, PlannerAgent executes approved or delegated actions. It orchestrates systems, workflows, and AI agents within defined authority, ensuring every action is governed and traceable.
PlannerAgent never owns decisions.
It ensures that:
- authority is explicit

CHARTER — Cognitive Authority Boundary
This layer does not make systems more autonomous. It does not assume that such systems should ever exist or be used.
It defines whether any cognitive system is allowed to hold decision authority at all.
It exists to ensure that:
no cognitive system can decide, allocate resources, or change policy without a formal, board-approved charter
autonomy levels are explicitly bounded, reviewable, and revocable
every cognitive system remains legally owned, human-sponsored, and continuously auditable
For most organizations, this layer remains inactive by design.
It is not needed to run, optimize, or improve operations.
It becomes active only if an organization considers granting a cognitive system decision authority beyond delegated execution — such as the ability to define objectives, modify policy, or allocate resources without direct human approval.
PlannerAgent does not introduce autonomous decision-makers.
It introduces a constitutional framework that determines whether any system is ever allowed to hold authority at all — and under which legal, organizational, and board-approved limits.
This layer is designed to support regulatory, legal, and board-level oversight of highly autonomous systems.
It is aligned with emerging AI governance and human-oversight regulatory frameworks.

The OAG defines:

delegation and budget authority

Every action in PlannerAgent — from using an AI tool (GRADUATE) to executing a process (SENIOR) or allocating improvement budget (PRINCIPAL) — is validated against this authority graph at runtime.
If a valid authority path does not exist, the system blocks the action by design.
This allows executives and boards to see, in real time, how responsibility, authority and impact propagate through the organization — not just on paper, but in operational reality.
